Introduction to Mathematics for Students of the Social Sciences.

What math foundations do you need as a student of the social sciences to learn to work comfortable with quantitative research and economic theories? In the first half of the course we looked at the basics of functions (linear and exponential), derivatives and integrals. In the second half, we studied basics of probability: Definition of a probability, measures of central tendency, dispersion and association.
